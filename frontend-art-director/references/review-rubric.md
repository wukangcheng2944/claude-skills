# Review Rubric

Use this rubric to make the review specific and repeatable.

## 1. Style coherence

Look for:
- clear tone and brand signal in the first screen
- disciplined typography
- restrained accent usage
- consistency in radii, borders, shadows, icon style, and illustration style
- a sense that the interface belongs to one system

Common failures:
- generic startup visuals
- too many treatments fighting each other
- premium aspirations with low-end visual decisions
- decoration substituting for identity

Questions to ask yourself:
- Would a user remember this product's feel after five seconds?
- Is the strongest text visually where it should be?
- Is the accent color being saved for emphasis or wasted everywhere?

## 2. Layout clarity

Look for:
- one dominant visual anchor
- clear reading order
- meaningful grouping
- stable spacing rhythm
- appropriate density for the task
- a strong relationship between headline, support content, and call to action

Common failures:
- card soup
- hierarchy inversion
- random whitespace
- important actions buried among secondary content
- everything occupying equal visual weight

Questions to ask yourself:
- Where does the eye land first?
- Is the next step obvious?
- Are sections composed intentionally or simply stacked?

## 3. Creativity

Look for:
- one memorable idea
- composition choices that feel chosen, not default
- a distinct narrative or visual thesis
- novelty that supports the goal instead of distracting from it

Common failures:
- safe templates with surface polish
- many little flourishes but no core idea
- copying references without translating them into this product's logic

Questions to ask yourself:
- What would someone remember here tomorrow?
- Is there a strong opening gesture or only competent assembly?

## 4. Reasonableness

Look for:
- fit to audience and use case
- realistic interaction density
- sensible responsiveness
- accessibility and contrast implications
- implementation realism for the proposed level of polish

Common failures:
- beautiful but impractical layouts
- overly sparse designs for dense workflows
- dense dashboards with no task hierarchy
- visual choices that are expensive to build but low in value

Questions to ask yourself:
- Does this solve the right problem for this screen?
- Would this still work on mobile, with real content, or with edge cases?
- Is the design ambition aligned with the product stage and resources?

## Severity guidance

Use severity language when helpful:
- critical: directly harms clarity, trust, or task completion
- major: noticeably weakens the page or product experience
- moderate: worth fixing after the major items
- minor: polish issue, not the main blocker
